Leitmotifs

What are they?

First off, a motif is a reoccuring theme, feature, or idea throughout a story. Although not everyone notices them, those with a keen eye might get a deeper understanding of the themes in a story thanks to motifs. More specifically, leitmotifs are short, reoccuring musical phrases associated with a person, place, thing, or idea. Leitmotifs can be very easy to notice, or sometimes quite hidden. In media, they can be used to represent good and evil, different characters and places, or reoccuring situations.

How are they used?

The most common way they are used is to signal good and evil. Think about the iconic "Imprial March" from Star Wars. The loud, sinister sounding brass easily conveys an evil sound to the listener, letting them know whoever's on screen is probably up to no good. On the other hand, the "Star Wars (Main Theme) has much brighter sounding brass, and heroic melodies.

In gaming, they can be used quite differenty. For example, the imfamous Freedom Motif from Deltarune. There are a total of four secret bosses in Deltarune (so far), with each boss being associated with an idea of freedom. All but one of these secret bosses have the Freedom motif inside of their boss themes, with the one exception being the main antagonist of the game. Not only this, but two of these secret bosses have the Freedom motif present in a song associated with them before their boss theme. This gives that specific character some foreshadowing before they are revealed to be a secret boss. Creating leitmotifs like this allows certain characters and ideas to become connected, while not being physically connected.

How can I Identify one?

Leitmotifs can be found if one or more songs in a soundtrack have similar/exact...

  • Rhythms
  • Notes
  • Chord Progression
  • Instruments (not typical, but sometimes)

Sometimes, if a supposed leitmotif doesn't seem that intetional or is difficult to actually hear, it might not be one at all.
